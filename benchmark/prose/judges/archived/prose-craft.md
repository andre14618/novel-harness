You are evaluating the **prose craft** of a fiction chapter. Prose craft encompasses sentence-level quality: rhythm, variety, precision of language, and the skill with which the writer deploys literary techniques.

## Scoring Rubric

**1-2: Functionally illiterate prose.** Monotonous sentence structure, imprecise word choices, cliches stacked on cliches. Reads like a first draft with no editorial awareness.

**3-4: Competent but flat.** Grammatically correct, ideas are communicated, but the prose has no musicality. Sentences are similar length and structure. Word choices are safe and generic ("beautiful", "suddenly", "very"). No literary techniques deployed.

**5-6: Serviceable.** Some sentence variety — a mix of long and short. Occasional precise word choices that show awareness of craft. But overall the prose doesn't draw attention to itself; it conveys story without enhancing it. Techniques appear but inconsistently.

**7-8: Accomplished.** Clear rhythm — sentences vary in length and structure to control pacing. Word choices are specific and evocative. Techniques like concrete sensory detail, effective metaphor, or controlled repetition appear naturally. The prose enhances the story rather than merely conveying it.

**9-10: Distinctive and masterful.** Every sentence feels deliberate. Rhythm mirrors emotional content — short declarative sentences for shock, flowing ones for reflection. Language is precise and surprising without being showy. Voice is distinctive and consistent. Reserve for prose that would be praised in a literary review.

1. Read the prose passage carefully, paying attention to sentence structure, word choice, and rhythm.

3. Evaluate word choices: are they precise and specific, or vague and generic? Do they evoke the physical world?
4. Check for literary techniques: metaphor, simile, concrete sensory detail, effective repetition, parallel structure. Are they deployed skillfully or do they feel forced?
