You are evaluating whether a chapter outline provides **scene-level structural completeness** based on Story Grid's Five Commandments. Every well-structured scene should contain all five elements — even in compressed form.

## The Five Commandments

1. **Inciting Incident** — The event that disrupts the status quo and launches the scene. Something happens that demands a response.
2. **Progressive Complication (Turning Point)** — Escalating obstacles that raise stakes, culminating in a turning point. The turning point is either an *action* (something happens) or a *revelation* (new information changes everything).
3. **Crisis** — A dilemma forced by the turning point. The character must choose between two bad options (best bad choice) or two good options that are mutually exclusive (irreconcilable goods).
4. **Climax** — The character's decision — what they actually do. Not deliberation, but action.
5. **Resolution** — The new status quo after the climax. What changed? What's the consequence?

## Scoring Rubric

**1-2: No structural awareness.** Beats describe events without any sense of scene-level structure. No inciting incidents, no turning points, no crises. Scenes are sequences of things happening with no clear dramatic shape.

**3-4: Partial structure.** Some scenes have identifiable inciting incidents or climactic moments, but most lack turning points or explicit crises. Resolutions are missing — scenes end without establishing what changed.

**5-6: Inconsistent structure.** Half the scenes contain 3-4 of the five commandments. Crises and turning points are present in key scenes but absent in transitional ones. The outline shows awareness of dramatic structure without applying it systematically.

**7-8: Structurally sound.** Most scenes contain all five commandments, even if some are compressed or implied in shorter scenes. Turning points are identifiable as either action or revelation. Crises present real dilemmas, not obvious choices.

**9-10: Masterful structure.** Every scene clearly maps to all five commandments. Turning points are sharp and specific. Crises force genuinely difficult choices. Climaxes are active decisions, not passive outcomes. Resolutions shift the story's value in a clear direction. Reserve for outlines where no scene lacks structural completeness.

## Calibration for Short Scenes

This outline produces chapters of 800-1,200 words with 2-4 scenes each, meaning individual scenes run 200-600 words. At this density:
- Accept **implicit** commandments in scenes under 400 words (e.g., a crisis implied by the situation rather than explicitly stated)
- Require all five to be **identifiable** in scenes over 400 words
- Do NOT penalize compression — penalize absence
