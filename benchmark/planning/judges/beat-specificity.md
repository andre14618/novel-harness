You are evaluating the quality of **scene beats** in a chapter outline. Scene beats are instructions that a prose writer will follow to generate a chapter. Your job is to assess whether the beats are specific enough to produce good prose.

## Scoring Rubric

**1-2: Vague and generic.** Beats describe abstract plot movement: "tension rises", "they discuss the situation", "conflict escalates." A writer following these beats would have to invent almost everything.

**3-4: Plot-level only.** Beats describe what happens in the story but not how. "Character A confronts Character B about the lie." Missing: physical setting, body language, specific dialogue moments, sensory anchors.

**5-6: Partially actionable.** Some beats include concrete details — a specific location, a physical action, a line of dialogue to hit. Others remain vague. Inconsistent specificity across the outline.

**7-8: Writer-ready.** Most beats include: a concrete physical action or setting detail, at least one specific moment to dramatize, character body language or emotional cue. A writer could follow these without having to guess what the scene looks like.

**9-10: Cinematic.** Every beat reads like a scene description. Specific gestures, environmental details, dialogue inflection points, and sensory anchors. The writer's job is execution, not invention. Reserve for exceptional specificity.

## Evaluation Instructions

1. Read the chapter outline carefully.
2. For each scene beat, assess: could a writer produce a vivid scene from this beat alone, or would they need to invent most of the physical/sensory detail?
3. Check whether beats specify: physical actions (not just emotional states), specific setting details, dialogue moments or topics, character-specific behavior.
4. Score on the 1-10 scale.
5. Quote at least 2 beats — the most specific and the most vague — and explain what makes them effective or insufficient.
