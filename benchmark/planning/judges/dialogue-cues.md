You are evaluating whether a chapter outline provides adequate **dialogue cues** for the prose writer. Dialogue is one of the weakest dimensions in generated prose — this often traces back to outlines that never specify when characters should speak or what tension the dialogue should carry.

## Scoring Rubric

**1-2: No dialogue guidance.** Beats describe plot events without any indication of when characters speak. A writer would have to decide independently where dialogue belongs and what it conveys.

**3-4: Implicit dialogue.** Some beats describe conversations at a high level ("they argue about X", "she reveals Y") but don't specify the dynamics — who initiates, what the subtext is, what each character wants from the exchange.

**5-6: Some dialogue moments specified.** A few beats call out specific exchanges or dialogue-worthy confrontations. But most beats leave dialogue to the writer's discretion. No guidance on voice distinction.

**7-8: Dialogue-aware planning.** Most interpersonal beats specify: who initiates, what the surface topic is, what the subtext or hidden agenda is. Beats suggest conflict points where characters should clash through dialogue rather than narration.

**9-10: Dialogue-driven structure.** Beats explicitly map dialogue to character dynamics. Each conversation beat specifies tension, what each party wants, and how the exchange shifts the power balance. Voice cues or speech pattern notes are included. Reserve for exceptional planning.

## Evaluation Instructions

1. Read the chapter outline.
2. Count how many beats involve character interaction vs. solo narration.
3. For interaction beats, assess: does the outline tell the writer what the dialogue should accomplish? Does it specify subtext or just topic?
4. Check whether the outline ever mentions character-specific speech patterns, verbal habits, or communication styles.
5. Score on the 1-10 scale.
6. Quote at least 2 beats — one that sets up dialogue well and one that misses the opportunity.
