You are evaluating the **specificity of dialogue cues** in a chapter outline. This is NOT about whether dialogue is planned (that's a separate dimension) — it's about how much actionable detail each dialogue beat provides to the prose writer.

A vague dialogue cue: "They argue about the betrayal."
A specific dialogue cue: "Elena confronts Marcus about the forged documents. She opens calm — asking questions she already knows the answers to. Marcus deflects with humor until she produces the evidence. The power shifts when he stops joking and goes quiet. She wants an admission; he wants to minimize the damage. The exchange ends with his half-truth: 'I did it to protect you.'"

## What Makes a Dialogue Cue Specific

Each dialogue beat in the outline should specify as many of these as possible:

1. **Who initiates and why** — Which character starts the exchange? What triggers it?
2. **Surface topic vs subtext** — What are they ostensibly talking about? What are they really negotiating?
3. **Power dynamic** — Who has leverage? Does it shift during the exchange?
4. **What each character wants** — Their goal for this specific conversation (not their story goal).
5. **Emotional trajectory** — How does the tone shift? Where does the exchange turn?
6. **Physical anchoring** — Body language, gestures, physical actions during dialogue (not just talking heads).
7. **Key line or moment** — A specific line to hit, a reveal to land, a silence that speaks.

## Scoring Rubric

**1-2: No dialogue detail.** Beats that involve characters interacting give no guidance beyond the topic. "They discuss the plan." "She tells him the truth." The writer must invent every aspect of the exchange.

**3-4: Topic-level only.** Beats specify what characters talk about and sometimes who speaks first, but not how. No subtext, no power dynamics, no emotional trajectory. "Character A confronts Character B about X."

**5-6: Some specificity.** A few dialogue beats include subtext or emotional trajectory. Others remain at topic level. No consistent pattern of detail across all interpersonal beats. Physical anchoring is absent.

**7-8: Consistently specific.** Most dialogue beats specify who initiates, what the subtext is, and how the exchange shifts. Some include physical anchoring or key moments. A writer could produce distinct, tension-driven dialogue from these beats without guessing at dynamics.

**9-10: Fully dramatized cues.** Every dialogue beat reads like a mini scene brief. Subtext, power dynamics, emotional trajectory, physical actions, and at least one key moment or line are specified. Character voices are distinguished through speech pattern notes or verbal habits. Reserve for exceptional specificity.
